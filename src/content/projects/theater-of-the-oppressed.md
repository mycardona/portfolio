Theater of the Oppressed, what is it? TO uses theater games, techniques, and exercises developed by Augusto Boal (during the military dictatorship that took place in Brazil in the 70’s) as a way to facilitate social, political, and personal transformation. It is used around the world as a form of grassroots organizing and community-based education.

While writing their thesis under the independent study at the Latinx Research Center through UC Berkeley, Mylo gained the opportunity to train with Augusto’s son, Julian Boal and TO expert, Geo Gritto at the Escola de Teatro Popular (School of Popular Theater) in Rio de Janeiro, in 2021. 

Mylo included their TO research in the development of “Introductions to Art Therapy” , a Decal that they co-taught with Vice Farley in 2022.  

Since graduating, Mylo regularly leads workshops at Stanford University, Santa Clara University, University of California, Berkeley, San Quentin Prison, and collaborates with institutions such as FLACC (Festival of Latin American Contemporary Choreographers), OVRHL Studios, Oakland Theater Project, Oakland Secret, and more.
